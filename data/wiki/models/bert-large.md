# BERT-Large

BERT-Large is the larger and more powerful variant of the BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) model, developed by Google AI Language.

## Overview

BERT-Large features 24 Transformer encoder layers, a hidden size of 1024, and 16 attention heads, resulting in approximately 340 million parameters. This larger size allows it to capture more complex patterns and achieve higher performance on NLP tasks compared to BERT-Base.

## Key Contributions / Capabilities

-   **Superior Performance:** Consistently outperformed BERT-Base and previous state-of-the-art models across a wide range of NLP benchmarks, demonstrating the benefits of increased model scale.
-   **State-of-the-Art Results:** Achieved leading results on tasks like GLUE, SQuAD v1.1, and SQuAD v2.0 upon its release.

## Connections

As the larger counterpart to BERT-Base, BERT-Large shares the same core architecture (Transformer encoder) and pre-training methodologies (MLM, NSP). Its success highlighted the importance of scaling up pre-trained models for NLP tasks.
